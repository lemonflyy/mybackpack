What kind of manners have I been taught?

From a young age, I’ve been taught to always call my parents’ friends auntie or uncle when greeting them. It is seen as polite and a way to show respect to your elders.
You’re also expected to greet any other family members when you meet them. As a child, it’s impolite to talk back to a person in a generation above you.

After a meal, it’s considered polite to say thank you to the person who made the meal. It’s a sign of appreciation and that the meal was well made.
Criticism is not welcome. It’s expected that you bring your dish to the sink so that someone can wash it.
hances are, if you ask for a specific thing to be made, you will be rejected. Only take the amount of food you are capable of finishing. Do not waste the food someone has prepared for you.

To make a good impression, just do things with etiquette and politeness.
