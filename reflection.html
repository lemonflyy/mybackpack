We often don't see things for what they are, we see them for what we are.

In other words, this means we don’t see something for what it is, but what we think it is. For example, person a could see person b as a weirdo, whereas, person c could see person b as normal.
Perhaps person a’s interests don’t align with person b so they just called them a weirdo. On the flip side, person c who saw person b as normal, probably has somewhat of the same interests as them.
It’s how a person perceives the world based on their interests and upbringing. Everything is based on how you perceive yourself, which you judge others based on that.
A person perceives the world based on who they are.
